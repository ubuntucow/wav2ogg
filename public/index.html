Frequently asked questions


What are WAV files?

Waveform Audio Files (WAVs) were created by Microsoft and IBM in 1991. WAVs are useful because they are uncompressed so they have great audio quality. However WAVs have a few disadvantages. Not only is the file size quite large compared to other formats, the WAV format is proprietary.


What are OGG files?

Ogg Vorbis Files (OGGs) are an open source alternative to WAV files. OGGs are much smaller in size whilst still maintaining most of the quality. Additionally support for OGG files has become extremely common in the past few years. They're very convenient for a variety of use cases because of their small file size.


How do I open WAV files?

Most operating systems have out of the box support for WAV. Simply double click a WAV file and it will open up in your default media player.


How do I open OGG files?

OGG files are also supported by nearly every operating system by default. Double click on a WAV file to listen to it in your default media player.
